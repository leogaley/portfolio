About

Thanks for visiting my portfolio! I am new to web development and really enjoying learning how front end and back end technologies work together. My favorite projects to work on are those where I can take a pragmatic approach to utilizing technology to support business needs. In other words, problem solving and business analysis is the driver for what technologies to use.

Since 2013 I have been working to try and create a very efficient and customized ERP system (Netsuite) for KC Store Fixtures. That includes the creation of hundreds of scripts, workflows, searches, and other customizations that are guided by business rules.

Adding front and back end web development to my toolkit is allowing me to pursue some areas that I have always wanted to. Continuous improvement of my own skillset is something I take great pride in, I love a challenge and I love problem-solving.

Graduate of University of Missouri, BS Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Engineering, MBA. I have a wife and one child, of course he is Leo M Galey V! I unapologetically play plenty of video games, plus watch and play as many sports as I have time for. As I get older I am pursuing becoming only a half-decent (but exuberant) golfer. I also enjoy reading all types of books, watching movies and documentaries. And now I pursue coding in my spare time as well.

Some unique life experiences have given me an (I think) uncommon and positive perspective on life - and I have a very, very supportive wife and family.

Full Stack App

This is an open-ended project created by a team of 4 in about 2 weeks (plus a little ongoing cleanup). We set out to create a mobile friendly version of 'Rotten Tomatoes for Games'. Just a few of the packages/libraries used are Bootstrap, jQuery, Node.js, Express.js, mySQL, Sequelize and Passport.js. The app is deployed to Heroku .

My role in this project was creating the front end HTML skeleton, authentication using Passport, site search functionality (including AutoComplete with jQuery.ui), and external API connections. We are very happy with the final outcome given the time constraints, and it was developed using a feature-based branching strategy on GitHub.
Check it out on GitHub
